But Who Can We Trust?

Anyone can post information on social media these days. It sure can be hard to find the right places to learn amidst the clutter. What makes your job easier is that there are reputable sources that have been around for years! Organisations like the Center for Disease Control (CDC) have long histories of providing accurate information!

It's also important to check research and information to see if the information was gathered correctly. We're not all experts at this, but scientists are! When you're looking for information, try to find it from scientists who have double-checked each others' work. Learn from them over politicians or journalists when possible. Some plaes to find kid-friendly science on COVID-19 are Scishow or Our World in Data.

Finally, try to double-check the information you find between different sources. Often, you'll notice that different organisations use slightly different statistics. For example, the World Health Organisation (WHO) might report different numbers for COVID-19 cases than the Center for Disease Control (CDC). That's fine, as long as both pieces of information are from reputable sources and not TOO far off. However, be careful of information that you can only find in one place or that is very different from other sources.

Stories on the Ground

Everyone is dealing with the pandemic differently, some worse than others. Doctors, employees, teachers, and citizens of developing countries are examples of those who've been hit hard. It's important to seek out and understand their perspectives. This is how we can connect all the information we learned about to the what's going on in people's day-to-day lives.

>

Developing Countries

  • India (the second-largest developing country in the world) has been hit especially hard by the pandemic.
  • Their public healthcare system has had difficulty treating patients. Doctors aren't receiving timely pay, and patients aren't receiving proper care.
>

Front-Line Workers

  • Lots of doctors are at risk of infection when exposed to COVID-19 patients
  • In some hospitals, there are many deceased patients. In others, the number of patients is almost 0 because people are scared to visit.
  • Doctors and nurses are working overtime
>

Informal Workers

  • The pandemic has cause many people to lose their jobs, especially in America.
  • Money is being sent to those who have lost their jobs.
  • Informal employees (minimum wage workers) are most affected
>

Educators

  • Classrooms and teaching will look extremely different because of COVID-19.
  • Lots of teachers are forced to come to school in-person, despite risks of infection.
